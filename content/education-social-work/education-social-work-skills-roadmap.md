## What Employers Look For in 2026

Education and social work careers are more regulated than most fields — you typically need specific degrees, certifications, and licenses before you can practice. This means the pathway is clearer but also more structured. Understanding exactly which qualifications you need, and in what sequence, prevents wasted time and money.

Here's a practical roadmap covering the essential credentials and skills for each career path.

## Teaching Certifications: India

**B.Ed (Bachelor of Education):** The foundational teaching qualification in India. B.Ed is a two-year program that covers pedagogy, educational psychology, curriculum development, and supervised classroom teaching practice. You need a bachelor's degree with at least 50% aggregate marks to enroll. The four-year Integrated B.Ed program combines a bachelor's degree with B.Ed, saving a year. B.Ed is mandatory for teaching in most government and recognized private schools.

**D.El.Ed (Diploma in Elementary Education):** A two-year diploma specifically for teaching at the primary level (classes 1-5). This is an alternative to B.Ed for primary school teaching and is accepted for CTET Paper 1 eligibility. D.El.Ed is offered at District Institutes of Education and Training (DIETs) and equivalent institutions.

**CTET (Central Teacher Eligibility Test):** A national eligibility test conducted by CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education). Passing CTET is mandatory for teaching positions in central government schools (Kendriya Vidyalayas, Navodaya Vidyalayas, and other schools under the central government). Paper 1 certifies you for primary teaching (classes 1-5), and Paper 2 certifies you for upper primary and secondary teaching (classes 6-8). The passing score is 60% (90 out of 150 marks), with a 5% relaxation for candidates from SC/ST/OBC and differently-abled categories. CTET scores are now valid for life (changed from the earlier seven-year validity).

**State TETs (Teacher Eligibility Tests):** Each state conducts its own TET for state government school positions. Requirements and exam patterns are similar to CTET but vary by state. For example, UPTET (Uttar Pradesh), TNTET (Tamil Nadu), KTET (Kerala), and HTET (Haryana) each have specific syllabi and eligibility criteria. You need to pass the relevant state TET to teach in that state's government schools.

**UGC NET (University Grants Commission National Eligibility Test):** The gateway to university-level teaching. UGC NET qualifies you for the position of Assistant Professor at Indian universities and colleges. Passing with a higher score also qualifies you for JRF (Junior Research Fellowship — a funded research position that provides a stipend of approximately ₹37,000 per month for two years, followed by ₹42,000 for the remaining three years). Eligibility: Master's degree with at least 55% marks (50% for SC/ST/OBC/PwD). The exam covers a general paper on teaching aptitude and research methodology, plus a subject-specific paper. UGC NET-qualified professors earn approximately ₹7.5 lakhs on average, compared to ₹4.5-5 lakhs without it.

**M.Ed (Master of Education):** A two-year postgraduate degree for those who want to specialize in educational leadership, curriculum development, teacher education, or educational research. M.Ed is increasingly valued for positions as teacher educators (faculty at B.Ed colleges), education administrators, and senior curriculum roles.

## Teaching Certifications: US

**State Teaching License:** The mandatory credential for teaching in public schools. Each of the 50 states has its own requirements, but the typical pathway involves a bachelor's degree (often in your teaching subject), completion of a state-approved teacher preparation program (which includes supervised student teaching), and passing required examinations.

**Praxis Exams:** The most widely used teacher certification exams in the US. Praxis Core tests foundational reading, writing, and mathematics skills (typical passing scores: Reading 156, Writing 162, Mathematics 150). Praxis Subject Assessments test content knowledge in your specific teaching area (biology, mathematics, English, social studies, etc.). Praxis CKT tests elementary curriculum knowledge. Scores are valid for 10 years. Exam costs range from $50-$200+ per test. Not all states use Praxis — some (like California, Texas, Florida, New York) have their own examination systems.

**Master's Degree:** Not required to begin teaching, but most school districts offer salary increases for teachers who earn a Master's degree — typically $5,000-$15,000 annually. Many teachers pursue Master's degrees in their subject area, in education, or in specialized fields like special education, STEM education, or educational leadership.

**National Board Certification:** The gold standard teaching credential in the US, offered by the National Board for Professional Teaching Standards (NBPTS). This voluntary certification involves a rigorous portfolio-based assessment process. Board-certified teachers often receive salary supplements ($2,000-$10,000 depending on the state and district).

## Social Work Certifications

**BSW (Bachelor of Social Work):** The entry-level social work degree. A four-year program covering human behavior, social policy, research methods, and supervised field placement. In India, BSW programs are offered at universities like TISS, Delhi School of Social Work, and many state universities. In the US, CSWE-accredited BSW programs qualify graduates for entry-level social service positions.

**MSW (Master of Social Work):** The professional degree for advanced social work practice. A two to three year graduate program with concentrations in clinical social work, community practice, policy, administration, or research. In the US, MSW is the minimum qualification for most independent social work practice. TISS Mumbai is India's premier MSW program, but many universities including Delhi University, Jamia Millia Islamia, and Loyola College Chennai offer strong programs.

**LCSW (Licensed Clinical Social Worker — US):** The clinical practice license that authorizes independent mental health practice. Requirements: MSW from a CSWE-accredited program, 3,000-4,000 hours of supervised clinical experience (typically 2-3 years post-MSW working under an experienced LCSW's supervision), and passing the ASWB Clinical Exam (administered by the Association of Social Work Boards — the exam costs $260 as of 2025). The LCSW credential delivers a 20-30% salary increase and enables private practice, insurance billing, and clinical leadership roles. The total timeline from starting your MSW to LCSW is typically 4-5 years.

**RCI Registration (India):** The Rehabilitation Council of India registers clinical psychologists and rehabilitation professionals. To register as a clinical psychologist, you need an MA in Clinical Psychology or (until 2025-26) an M.Phil in Clinical Psychology. Note: RCI regulates clinical psychology and rehabilitation-related counseling — general school counselors and educational counselors whose work doesn't focus on disability fall outside RCI's regulatory scope. Registration must be completed within six months of course completion.

**LPC/LMHC (US):** Licensed Professional Counselor or Licensed Mental Health Counselor — state-level credentials for counseling professionals who are not social workers. Requirements vary by state but typically include a Master's degree in counseling, 2,000-4,000 hours of supervised experience, and passing the NCE (National Counselor Examination) or NCMHCE (National Clinical Mental Health Counseling Examination).

## Special Education Certifications

**In India:** The RCI requires specific qualifications for special educators — typically a B.Ed in Special Education or a Diploma in Special Education (D.S.E) in areas like visual impairment, hearing impairment, intellectual disability, or learning disabilities. The National Institute for the Empowerment of Persons with Multiple Disabilities (NIEPMD) and similar national institutes offer specialized training programs.

**In the US:** Special education teachers need a state teaching license with a special education endorsement. Most states require specific coursework in special education law, assessment, IEP development (Individualized Education Program — a legally mandated plan that specifies educational goals and services for each student with a disability), behavior management, and adaptive instruction. Many teachers add special education certification to an existing general teaching license.

## Technology Skills for Modern Education

Technology competency has shifted from optional to essential. Here are the key technology skills for education and social work professionals.

**Learning Management Systems (LMS):** Platforms like Google Classroom, Canvas, Moodle, and Blackboard that manage course content, assignments, grading, and student communication. Teachers and instructional designers need proficiency with at least one major LMS. Google Classroom is the most widely used free platform; Canvas dominates US higher education.

**EdTech and Digital Content Tools:** Canva for creating visual learning materials. Kahoot, Quizlet, and Mentimeter for interactive assessments and engagement. Nearpod and Pear Deck for interactive presentations. Video creation tools (OBS Studio, Loom, Screencast-O-Matic) for flipped classroom content (a teaching model where students watch instructional videos at home and do practice activities in class, reversing the traditional homework-lecture sequence).

**AI Tools in Education:** An estimated 86% of education organizations now use generative AI, and 61% of teachers use AI in their work (up from 32% the previous year). Key applications include AI-assisted lesson planning, automated grading for objective assessments, personalized learning recommendations, and language translation for multilingual classrooms. Understanding how to use AI tools effectively and ethically — including their limitations and potential for bias — is an emerging essential skill.

**Data Analysis for Education:** Basic skills in analyzing student performance data, assessment results, and learning outcomes. Comfort with spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets), data visualization, and understanding of basic statistical concepts (mean, median, standard deviation, correlation). Schools increasingly use data-driven instruction — adjusting teaching based on assessment data rather than assumptions.

**Case Management Software (Social Work):** Social workers use platforms like Social Solutions (Apricot, ETO), Penelope, and CaseWorthy for managing client records, tracking service delivery, and generating outcome reports. Government agencies use systems like SACWIS (Statewide Automated Child Welfare Information System) for child welfare case management.

## Foundation Skills Across Both Fields

**Assessment and evaluation:** The ability to assess needs — whether a student's learning level or a client's support requirements — and evaluate whether interventions are working. This includes designing assessments, interpreting results, and adjusting approaches based on evidence.

**Cultural competence and inclusivity:** Working effectively with people from diverse linguistic, cultural, socioeconomic, and ability backgrounds. In India's multilingual landscape (22 official languages, hundreds of dialects), this includes ability to communicate and connect across language barriers. In the US, cultural competence includes understanding the experiences of immigrant communities, racial and ethnic minorities, and LGBTQ+ populations.

**Ethics and professional boundaries:** Both fields involve positions of trust and power — over children, over vulnerable populations. Understanding and maintaining professional boundaries, navigating ethical dilemmas, maintaining confidentiality, and recognizing your own limits and biases are non-negotiable professional skills.

**Self-care and emotional resilience:** Burnout rates in teaching and social work are higher than most professions. Developing practices for emotional regulation, stress management, peer support, and professional supervision protects your effectiveness and longevity in the field.

## The 90-Day Quick-Start Plan

**Weeks 1-3:** Clarify your direction. Read about different roles within education and social work. Talk to two or three working professionals. Identify whether you're drawn to classroom teaching, counseling, community social work, EdTech, or another path. This clarity determines your qualification path.

**Weeks 4-6:** Begin the credential pathway. If targeting teaching in India, research B.Ed program admissions (entrance exams, deadlines, program quality). If targeting social work, research BSW/MSW programs. If targeting the US, research teacher preparation programs and state licensing requirements. Start preparing for entrance exams if applicable.

**Weeks 7-9:** Gain direct experience. Volunteer as a tutor, teaching assistant, or community volunteer. Organizations like Teach For India, Pratham, CRY, or local community centers offer structured volunteer opportunities. Even 5-10 hours per week of direct experience helps you understand whether the daily work energizes or drains you.

**Weeks 10-12:** Build foundational skills. Complete a free online course on pedagogy (Coursera offers courses from top universities), educational psychology, or social work principles. Start developing technology skills — learn Google Classroom or Canva, explore AI tools for education. Join professional communities on LinkedIn or Facebook groups for teachers and social workers in your area.

These fields reward genuine commitment to people and communities. The qualification pathways are well-defined, the demand is consistent, and the impact is tangible. Start by confirming that the daily work — not just the idea of the work — energizes you, then follow the credential pathway systematically.
